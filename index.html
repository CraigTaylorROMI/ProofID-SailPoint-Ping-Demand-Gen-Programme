Data & lists

  • Inputs: Ping and SailPoint installed base lists (in-scope criteria applied).
  • Enrichment: fill gaps for contact roles, seniority, business unit, region, and account metadata.
  • HubSpot readiness: structured properties for segmentation and reporting.
  • Handover: “ready-to-work” target lists for SDR + LinkedIn (HeyReach upload).

SME interviews

  • Purpose: validate the real optimisation/value-realisation problems and language buyers use.
  • Outputs: content angles, proof points, and assessment question set.
  • Format: 30–45 mins per SME, structured question guide, recorded notes.
  • Decision: finalise what the “Assessment debrief” covers so SDR can sell it confidently.

Cadence, people, and trackers

  • Weekly: optimisation review (what changed, what we do next).
  • Fortnightly: content + backlog prioritisation.
  • Monthly: exec view (progress vs outcomes, risks, next bets).
  • People needed: SDR owner, 1–2 technical SMEs, marketing ops (HubSpot), Proof social supporters.
  • Trackers: timeline, costs, asset status, channel performance, SDR outcomes.
  • Costs: we’ll add ballparks once the plan is locked.

Channels

We’ll lead with activity across five channels and keep them joined up around a single conversion path: The Value Realisation Assessment → assessment debrief call (not a generic discovery call).

Channel plan

SDR (HubSpot Sales Workspace)

Priority channel for converting high-intent engagement into booked meetings.

Impact: High Timeline: ASAP
  • Setup: SDR working in HubSpot Sales Workspace with enriched Ping + SailPoint installed-base data.
  • Outreach assets: email templates that reuse programme content + a call script for the Assessment.
  • Operating model: SDR calls + 1:1 emails in HubSpot; prioritise based on engagement + signals.
  • Phase 2: consider Prospecting Agent for semi-automated outbound once core messaging is proven.
Output: booked “Assessment Debrief” calls + feedback loop to improve messaging.

LinkedIn (SDR-led + coordinated Proof support)

Used to expand reach, increase familiarity, and improve connection + reply rates.

Impact: High Timeline: ASAP
  • Profile optimisation: make SDR profile relevant to ICP and add conversion assets (Assessment link).
  • HeyReach: upload installed-base contacts; send ~20 connection requests/day per SDR account.
  • DM cadence: write 3 automated messages for new connections (cadence agreed in build).
  • Posting: SDR posts 1–2×/week using programme themes to build social proof and lift accept rates.
  • Paid: use LinkedIn ads (matched audiences) to ensure top-of-funnel content reaches the full list.
  • Amplification: coordinate active Proof people to support engagement (likes/comments/saves/reposts).
Output: higher-quality conversations and clicks into the Assessment landing page.

Website (Assessment-first conversion path)

Make the Assessment the most obvious “next step” across relevant site journeys.

Impact: High Timeline: ASAP
  • Landing page: dedicated page positioning the Value Realisation Assessment (Ping + SailPoint variants).
  • Promotion: site CTAs + pop-ups to drive visibility and conversions.
  • Internal linking: route from relevant pages + blogs into the Assessment landing page.
  • Tracking: ensure HubSpot capture of visits, repeat visits, and Assessment starts/completions.
Output: Assessment completions and clear signals for SDR prioritisation.

Email (installed base warming + nurture)

Build familiarity over time and reinforce key themes that support SDR conversion.

Impact: Medium Timeline: Phase 2
  • Newsletters: one monthly for Ping, one monthly for SailPoint (expect low initial open/click; focus on warming).
  • Enable SDR: repurpose content into 1:1 templates and short sequences where appropriate.
  • Automation: once performing, add SDR-initiated sequences + Prospecting Agent where it improves consistency.
Output: increased familiarity + repeated exposures to the Assessment offer.

LLMs (AEO) via Searchable

Create content structured to be found and referenced when buyers use LLMs to research optimisation questions.

Impact: Medium Timeline: Phase 2
  • Question mining: use Searchable to identify common questions about Ping/SailPoint value realisation.
  • LLM-first blogs: write and structure posts to answer the question clearly (and link into the Assessment).
  • Measurement: track assisted conversions (blog → Assessment visit/start) rather than raw traffic vanity metrics.
Output: incremental high-intent visits and “assist” conversions into the Assessment path.

How the channels connect (simple operating loop)

Every channel pushes toward the same next step — then we use signals to prioritise follow-up.

Design: joined-up Starts: ASAP
  • Reach: LinkedIn organic + paid + email warming increases exposure across the installed base.
  • Convert: website landing page + Assessment provide a clear action and collect intent signals.
  • Prioritise: HubSpot signals/score flags “worth a call” accounts for the SDR.
  • Learn: SDR outcomes feed back into content, messaging, and scoring thresholds.
Output: fewer “cold” conversations and more assessment-led meetings.

What improves as familiarity builds

  • Connection accepts + replies improve as the SDR looks familiar and content feels consistently relevant.
  • Assessment starts rise as the Value Realisation Assessment is seen repeatedly (not once).
  • Calls booked improve when “Scorecard Results Review” is clear, specific, and valuable.
  • SQL rate increases as accounts show multiple personas + multiple intent categories.

Q1 ramp management

  • January: lists + scoring live, conversion assets live, outreach starts.
  • February: expand reach (paid + HeyReach), tighten messaging based on SDR feedback.
  • March: prioritise high-intent accounts, push completion → call booking.
  • Weekly: optimisation loop (what moved, what didn’t, what we change).
